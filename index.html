03 · purpose & scope 03 / 22
internal SOP reference
scope of this SOP

What this procedure covers, and what it doesn't.

This SOP governs internal quality control (IQC) for antimicrobial susceptibility testing (AST) by disc diffusion and applies to all technologists performing AST in the Microbiology section.

In scope Kirby-Bauer disc diffusion QC, QC strain handling, disc/media storage, zone reading, range verification, corrective action.
Out of scope MIC/broth microdilution QC, automated AST platform QC, EQA scheme administration (covered under separate SOPs).
04 · standards 04 / 22
ISO 15189:2022 · CLSI
why we follow these

Every IQC decision in this SOP traces back to a clause, not a habit.

ISO 15189:2022 Clause 6.6 requires verification that examination procedures perform as intended before patient results are released. CLSI defines what "as intended" means numerically.

  • ISO 15189:2022 · 6.6Control of externally provided products and services / examination QC.
  • CLSI M02Performance standards for antimicrobial disc susceptibility tests.

07 · strain handling 07 / 22
subculture discipline
keeping strains true

QC strains drift too. Subculturing discipline is part of the control.

Working stocks are subcultured from frozen/lyophilised reference stock, never serially passaged beyond the validated limit, to avoid phenotypic drift.

  • Reference stockFrozen at -80°C, never used directly for daily QC.
  • Working stockSubcultured weekly from reference; maximum 5 passages before discard.
  • Daily plateFresh subculture each testing day, 18-24h growth, purity-checked.

10 · disc handling 10 / 22
potency depends on storage
where potency is won or lost

Discs lose potency before they look any different.

Stock supply -20°C, desiccated, sealed until needed.
In-use cartridge 2-8°C in dispenser, equilibrated to room temp before opening, used within manufacturer shelf life.
Handling rule Never refreeze once thawed. Discard if condensation is seen inside the cartridge.
11 · inoculum 11 / 22

density before plating

Inoculum density is the single most common silent error.

Suspension is adjusted to 0.5 McFarland turbidity standard, verified against a densitometer where available, and plated within 15 minutes of preparation.

14 · zone reading 14 / 22
measure to the nearest mm
where reading errors creep in

Read against a dark background, reflected light, caliper or ruler on the underside of the plate.

Common errors: reading through the lid, including the haze of light growth within the zone, or rounding inconsistently between technologists.

  • Edge of clear inhibitionMeasured to the point growth resumes, not the disc edge.
  • Swarming organismsProteus spp.: ignore the swarm veil, read the obvious zone.
  • Double zonesRead the outer zone unless the SOP for that drug specifies otherwise.

20 · documentation 20 / 22

the audit trail

Records are what turn a daily habit into evidence of competence.

Every QC run, pass or fail, is logged the same day, with the technologist's initials, on the designated QC log or LIMS module.

  • QC log entryDate, organism, disc lot, media lot, zone diameter, in/out of range.
  • New lot verificationRecorded separately before the lot enters routine use.
  • NCE / CAPA recordLinked to the QC log entry that triggered it.
21 · constraints & limitations 21 / 22
factors that may affect IQC performance
constraints & limitations

Some factors lie outside the technologist's direct control.

Supply chain variability Inconsistent availability of ATCC reference strains and certified disc cartridges in resource-limited settings may interrupt routine QC schedules.
Cold chain reliability Power interruptions affecting refrigeration and freezer units can compromise disc potency and QC strain viability before any visible sign of degradation.
Staffing & training continuity High staff turnover and limited refresher training opportunities can introduce inter-technologist variability in inoculum preparation and zone reading.
CLSI edition currency Delays in accessing the latest CLSI M100 edition mean QC ranges may lag behind updated breakpoints, affecting interpretive accuracy.
